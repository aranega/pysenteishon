How to use Pysenteishon in 5 steps

Step 1

Run Pysenteishon in a terminal

Step 2

Open http://localhost:5000 at your web browser and click on Network settings

Step 3

With a web browser, open on your smartphone the url provided at Network settings

Step 4

Go to your laptop, open your slides, and put them in presentation mode (press F5)

Step 5

Swipe your finger at your touchscreen, the slides should move!

Pysenteishon for Windows Vista/7/8

  1. Download Pysenteishon.exe
  2. Double click Pysenteishon.exe, you will be asked to choose a directory where to extract
  3. Go to the extracted directory and execute pysenteishon.bat . A windows command terminal (black screen with text) will be executed, leave it open since if you close this windows, you will close Pysenteishon.If the windows firewall ask you to allow connection, say yes.
  4. Open your web browser and go to: http://127.0.0.1:5000 . You will be given an url you need to connect from your smartphone.
  5. Open a web browser in your smartphone and go to the url provided in the step before
  6. Open your slides with the software you prefer and put them in presentation mode (for example in Powerpoint is F5) . You can find a sample presentation to try here
  7. Thats all! Swipe your finger at your smartphone touchscreen! To close Pysenteishon, simple close the windows command terminal (black screen with text)

  2. Untar pysenteishon tar -zxvf pysenteishon-linux-x86_64-3.4.tar.gz
  3. Change directory into the extracted directory cd pysenteishon-linux-x86_64-3.4

  5. Open your web browser and go to: http://127.0.0.1:5000 . Click on Network settings, You will be given an url you need to connect from your smartphone. Be sure to allow connections on port 5000.
  6. Open a web browser in your smartphone and go to the url provided in the step before
  7. Open your slides with the software you prefer and put them in presentation mode (for example in Powerpoint is F5) . You can find a sample presentation to try here

Pysenteishon

Gives you skills to let you change your presentation slides with your smartphone!

About Pysenteishon

It's a simple Python application that lets you control your presentation slides with the swipe of a finger on your phone's touchscreen.

How it works

It's a web based application that listens on port 5000 (by default). When you open your smartphone (using a standard web browser) and navigate to the url, your finger swipes are captured by the cool javascript library TouchSwipe. Then, thanks to the excellent PyUserInput python library, each javascript swipe event calls a specific url that emulates a keyboard tap.

How to use it

Run pysenteishon on your laptop. If you have a firewall running, make sure you allow connections on port 5000. Then open your presentation with the software you're used to using (like Powerpoint), and make sure that application is in focus. On your smartphone, open the url given by pysenteishon (click on Network settings). Swipe your finger on the touchscreen to control your slides and start your talk/presentation!
